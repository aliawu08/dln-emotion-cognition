# Greenwald et al. (2009) DLN Stage Coding Rubric

## Source
Greenwald, A. G., Poehlman, T. A., Uhlmann, E. L., & Banaji, M. R. (2009).
Understanding and using the Implicit Association Test: III. Meta-analysis of
predictive validity. *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 97(1), 17–41.

## Unit of Analysis
**Independent sample** (k=184). Study-level data extracted from the Appendix of
Greenwald et al. (2009). Each sample is coded by its criterion-domain topic using
the decision tree below. Sensitivity analyses compare results with and without
mixed/unclear domains.

## Coding Target
What is being coded: the **criterion behavior** that the IAT is predicting, not
the IAT itself (which is the same instrument across domains).

## Decision Tree

### Step 1: Is the criterion behavior reflexive or automatic?
Does the criterion involve immediate, stimulus-bound reactions with minimal
deliberation (e.g., approach-avoidance motor responses, startle responses,
snap consumer judgments, physiological reactivity)?

- **Yes → Dot**
- No → Step 2

### Step 2: Is the criterion behavior deliberative with social-desirability pressure?
Does the criterion involve self-regulated behavior where social norms,
self-presentation motives, or institutional pressures could suppress
explicit-implicit alignment (e.g., intergroup discrimination in public,
hiring decisions, political behavior under social scrutiny)?

- **Yes → Linear**
- No → Step 3

### Step 3: Does the criterion involve well-practiced integrative behavior?
Does the criterion require sustained relational engagement, self-knowledge,
or context-dependent strategy (e.g., close-relationship dynamics, therapeutic
alliance, professional judgment in familiar domains)?

- **Yes → Network**
- If none of the above → **Mixed/Unclear** (document rationale)

## Pre-specified Domain Assignments (applied to k=184 samples)

| Appendix Topic | DLN Stage | k | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer | Dot | 40 | Snap judgments; approach-avoidance; low deliberation |
| Race (Bl/Wh) | Linear | 32 | Intergroup discrimination; social desirability suppresses explicit |
| Politics | Linear | 11 | Social identity and norms create explicit-implicit divergence |
| Gender/sex | Linear | 15 | Intergroup behavior; social desirability pressure |
| Other intergroup | Linear | 15 | Intergroup discrimination; rule-governed self-monitoring |
| Relationships | Network | 12 | Sustained relational integration; high self-knowledge |
| Personality | Mixed | 16 | May span Dot (trait-automatic) and Network (self-awareness) |
| Drugs/tobacco | Mixed | 24 | Clinical/health: varies by specific criterion behavior |
| Clinical | Mixed | 19 | Clinical/health: phobic avoidance may be Dot; health behavior may be Linear |

## Theoretical Predictions by Stage

- **Dot domains**: Moderate IAT validity (both IAT and criterion tap reactive
  processes); explicit measures show low validity (not tapping the right level).
- **Linear domains**: Largest explicit-implicit discrepancy; moderate IAT validity;
  low explicit validity (compartmentalization suppresses alignment).
- **Network domains**: Smallest explicit-implicit gap; both IAT and explicit
  measures predict criterion (integration aligns implicit and explicit).

## Ambiguous Cases
- **Clinical/health**: Code as Mixed/Unclear unless the specific criterion
  behavior can be classified. Run sensitivity analyses with and without.
- **Personality**: May span Dot (trait-automatic) and Network (self-awareness).
  Document and run with/without.

## Blinding Protocol
Coders should code domain descriptions from the Greenwald et al. method sections
without seeing the reported IAT-criterion correlations. Merge with effect sizes
after coding is complete.

## Inter-rater Reliability
- Two independent coders
- Target: Cohen's kappa >= 0.70
- Resolve disagreements by discussion; document final decision rationale
- If kappa < 0.60, recalibrate rubric before proceeding
